EXT. LUNAR SURFACE – DAY

The vast, grey SILENCE of the moon. Dust motes hang impossibly still in the weak light. CAPTAIN KIRA (40s, sharp, tired) walks toward a ruined DOME, her footsteps crunching in the dust. She pauses, squinting at the horizon.

In the distance, a figure — small, unsteady — appears against the glare. It moves slowly, dragging something metallic behind it. The sound is faint but rhythmic, like breathing through a machine.

INT. COMMAND CENTER – NIGHT

The control room hums softly. Screens flicker with endless streams of data. LIEUTENANT MORO leans over a console, eyes wide, speaking to someone over the comms.

MORO (urgent) Captain, we’re losing signal! The dome’s shielding is stronger than we thought.

KIRA (V.O.) Keep trying. I’m almost inside.

EXT. LUNAR DOME – DAY

Kira reaches the entrance — a fractured steel hatch half-buried in dust. She brushes her gloved hand over the surface, revealing faded lettering: PROJECT STARLIGHT.

Her eyes narrow. A faint reflection of her helmet visor glows in the metal — a second shadow moves just behind her.
